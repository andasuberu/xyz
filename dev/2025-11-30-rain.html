Rain

2025-11-30 • 1 min read

If you've lived here long enough, you've probably stood under a roof waiting for the rain to stop. Because there was nothing else you could do.

The rain doesn't ask whether you're running late, it doesn't care that you're only five minutes from home. When it starts, everyone makes the same decision.

Wait.

Strangers become temporary neighbors, everyone checks the sky every few minutes, somebody jokes that it's only getting started, somebody else insists it will stop soon.

For me there's something strangely pleasant about those moments.

For a few minutes, everyone's schedule is paused.

The office worker.

The student.

The bike man.

We're all standing under the same roof, waiting for the same rain.

Eventually, someone decides they've waited long enough and runs for it.

Then another.

And another.

A few minutes later the rain begins to ease and everyone is free to leave.

Life is a little like that.

Those rainstorms remind us that not everything can be hurried.

Sometimes all you can do is wait, chat with the stranger next to you, watch the road disappear beneath the rain, and trust that the sky will eventually make room for sunshine again.

It always does.
