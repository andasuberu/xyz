The Rain Will Pass

2025-11-30 • 1 min read

If you've lived here long enough, you've probably stood under a roof waiting for the rain to stop.

Not because you wanted to.

Because there was nothing else you could do.

The rain doesn't ask whether you're running late.

It doesn't care that you're only five minutes from home.

When it starts, everyone makes the same decision.

Wait.

Strangers become temporary neighbors.

Everyone checks the sky every few minutes.

Somebody jokes that it's only getting started.

Somebody else insists it will stop soon.

Nobody really knows either of those things.

There's something comforting about those moments.

For a few minutes, everyone's schedule is paused.

The office worker.

The student.

The okada rider.

We're all standing under the same roof, waiting for the same rain.

Eventually, someone decides they've waited long enough and runs for it.

Someone else stays behind.

A few minutes later, the rain begins to ease.

It usually does.

Life is a little like that.

Storms don't always disappear quickly.

They remind us that not everything can be hurried.

Sometimes all you can do is wait, chat with the stranger next to you, watch the road disappear beneath the rain, and trust that the sky will eventually make room for sunshine again.

It always does.
